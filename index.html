About Me

I am Francis Chileshe, a final-year Computer Science student at the University of Zambia (UNZA) with a strong academic interest in Networking and Information Security and a growing passion for web development. I enjoy designing and building web pages that are clean, responsive, and easy to use across different devices. Through my studies and practical work, I have developed a solid foundation in HTML5, CSS3, semantic structure, responsive design, and modern layout techniques such as Flexbox and CSS Grid.

I am particularly interested in how good web design goes beyond appearance and also includes usability, accessibility, performance, and maintainability. My background in security also shapes the way I think about development, because I believe digital systems should be built with reliability, structure, and trust in mind from the start. This has helped me appreciate the importance of writing organized code, planning layouts carefully, and testing designs across screen sizes.

As I continue improving my front-end skills, I also want to deepen my knowledge of secure systems, infrastructure, and software quality. My long-term goal is to become a well-rounded computing professional who can build solutions that are not only visually effective, but also dependable, user-friendly, and secure.
